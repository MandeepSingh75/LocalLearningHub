Daily Vocabulary
Persevere
verb
To continue doing something despite difficulty or delay in achieving success.
"She persevered through months of hard work to finally pass her exam."
Eloquent
adjective
Fluent and persuasive in speaking or writing; clearly expressing ideas.
"His eloquent speech moved everyone in the room."
Diligent
adjective
Having or showing care and conscientiousness in one's work or duties.
"A diligent student always reviews their notes after class."
Inquisitive
adjective
Having a desire to know or learn about many things; curious.
"An inquisitive mind is the most powerful tool a learner can have."
Articulate
adjective / verb
Having or showing the ability to speak fluently and coherently.
"She could articulate her ideas clearly even under pressure."
Belligerent
Adjective (can also be used as a Noun)

Hostile, aggressive, or engaged in a war. Synonyms: Pugnacious, antagonistic, combative.

""The belligerent tone of the ambassador’s speech suggested that diplomatic talks were failing.""
Skirmish
Noun (can also be used as a Verb)

A brief or minor fight between small groups of soldiers, usually away from the main battle.

Synonyms: Scuffle, fray, clash.

""The two armies engaged in a brief skirmish near the border, but no major casualties were reported.""
Subjugate
Verb

To bring under complete control or subjection; to conquer by force.

Synonyms: Vanquish, overpower, enthrall.

""The invading empire sought to subjugate the neighboring islands and exploit their natural resources.""

collocations for essay writing

  • Play a crucial role
    Meaning: To have a very important effect or influence on something.
    Example: Technology plays a crucial role in modern education.

  • Pose a serious threat
    Meaning: To cause a significant danger or risk.
    Example: Global warming poses a serious threat to marine life.

  • Reach a consensus
    Meaning: To come to an agreement after discussion.
    Example: The government and experts must reach a consensus on new policies.

  • Widen the gap
    Meaning: To increase the difference between two groups or situations.
    Example: Poor education systems can widen the gap between social classes.

  • Take responsibility for
    Meaning: To accept blame or duty for something.
    Example: Companies should take responsibility for environmental damage.

    Why I Started Writing Every Day
    Writing every day was not something I planned. It started simply — I wanted to remember things better. I noticed that when I wrote something down, it stayed with me longer than when I just thought about it. So I decided to give it a real try.

    The first few days were hard. I did not know what to write, and the words did not come easily. But I kept going. Slowly, I began to enjoy it. Writing helped me think more clearly. It helped me understand my own thoughts.

    Now I see writing not just as practice, but as a habit that makes me a better thinker. And that, I believe, is worth every difficult first sentence.
